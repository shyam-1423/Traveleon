Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed between 1632 and 1653, this UNESCO World Heritage site is renowned for its breathtaking architectural beauty and intricate craftsmanship, symbolizing eternal love and one of the most iconic examples of Mughal architecture.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Winds," is an iconic pink sandstone structure in Jaipur, India. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, it features 953 small windows, or "jharokhas," designed to allow royal women to observe street festivals without being seen. The five-story palace's unique honeycomb design and ornate latticework make it a stunning example of Rajput architecture.
