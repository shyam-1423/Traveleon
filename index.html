Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. Constructed between 1632 and 1653, this UNESCO World Heritage site is renowned for its breathtaking architectural beauty and intricate craftsmanship, symbolizing eternal love and one of the most iconic examples of Mughal architecture.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is an iconic iron lattice tower located in Paris, France. Completed in 1889 and designed by Gustave Eiffel, it stands 324 meters tall and is one of the most recognizable structures in the world. Initially criticized, it has become a global cultural icon and symbol of French art and engineering. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of Paris from its three observation decks.
